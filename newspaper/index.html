Historic Climate Agreement Reached at Global Summit

World leaders commit to unprecedented carbon reduction targets by 2030
By Eleanor Vance • FROM OUR BUREAU IN PARIS • UPDATED 2 HOURS AGO
Global leaders at the Climate Summit in Paris yesterday

In a landmark decision that could shape the future of the planet, representatives from 195 countries have agreed to the most ambitious climate targets ever set. The agreement, reached after marathon negotiations that stretched into the early hours, commits signatories to reduce carbon emissions by 55% below 1990 levels by 2030.

The historic accord includes binding provisions for developed nations to provide $100 billion annually in climate financing to developing countries, a point of contention that nearly derailed the talks on multiple occasions.

"

This is not just an agreement on paper—it's a commitment to our children's future. The science is clear, and the time for action is now.

— Dr. Aisha Chen, Lead Negotiator

Environmental groups have praised the agreement as "a turning point in human history," while industry representatives expressed cautious optimism about the economic implications of the rapid transition to renewable energy sources.

The agreement comes as climate scientists report that global temperatures have already risen 1.2°C above pre-industrial levels, with devastating effects including more frequent and intense wildfires, hurricanes, and sea-level rise.
